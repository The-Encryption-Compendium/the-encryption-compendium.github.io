Encryption policy a crowd pleaser - ProQuest

Authors: Jeff Leiper

Published: November 1998

URL: http://search.proquest.com/docview/217686754?pq-origsite=summon

Abstract: At a meeting earlier this year by concerned industry players, the government was told that proposed restrictions were impractical because criminals had access to offshore encryption products. Further, the wait for a concrete policy was hurting business because companies were unsure of what they’d be allowed to sell. Shipping delays were costing money, officials from companies like kyberpass Corp. insisted. At the time, officials weren’t entirely frustrated. Timestep Corp.‘s Tim Hember opined that government would take their arguments seriously. ‘Their primary interest is the promotion of business. I’m bullish,’ he said.
